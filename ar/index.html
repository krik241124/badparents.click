Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about Bad Parenting

What is Bad Parenting?

Bad Parenting is a psychological horror game that follows the story of Ron, a young protagonist who encounters Mr. Redface, a mysterious figure that influences family dynamics in disturbing ways.

How long does it take to complete the game?

Bad Parenting is designed to be completed in approximately 20-30 minutes, offering a concise but impactful experience that can be finished in one sitting.

Is the game suitable for all ages?

No, Bad Parenting contains mature themes including psychological horror, family trauma, and disturbing situations. It is recommended for mature audiences only.

What platforms is the game available on?

Bad Parenting is currently available on Windows PC through Steam and itch.io platforms.

What type of gameplay can I expect?

The game features exploration-based gameplay with a focus on narrative and atmosphere. Players will navigate through environments, interact with objects, and uncover the story through environmental storytelling.

Who is Mr. Redface?

Mr. Redface is the central antagonist of the game, a mysterious figure that represents the darker aspects of family relationships and parental influence.

Are there multiple endings?

No, Bad Parenting features a single, focused narrative path that delivers a powerful and cohesive story experience.

What are the system requirements?

The game has modest requirements: Windows 10/11, 4GB RAM, and a DirectX 11 compatible graphics card. It's designed to run smoothly on most modern PCs.

Is there combat in the game?

No, Bad Parenting focuses on exploration and storytelling rather than combat mechanics. The horror elements are psychological rather than action-based.

What makes the game unique?

Bad Parenting stands out for its unique blend of retro-inspired graphics, psychological horror elements, and its thoughtful exploration of family dynamics and trauma through a short but impactful narrative.

Meet the Cast: A Psychological Horror Ensemble

Enter a world where every character plays a crucial role in weaving a narrative of psychological horror and family trauma. Each character represents different aspects of the game's dark themes:

Ron

The young protagonist whose perspective we experience throughout the game. Ron's journey through this psychological nightmare reflects the impact of toxic family dynamics on a child's psyche. His interactions with Mr. Redface and the environment reveal deeper layers of family trauma and psychological horror.

Mr. Redface

The enigmatic antagonist whose presence pervades the game's atmosphere. Mr. Redface serves as a manifestation of parental authority gone wrong, representing the psychological impact of toxic family relationships. His mysterious nature and unsettling appearance create a constant sense of dread throughout the narrative.

The Family

The supporting characters in Ron's family each contribute to the narrative's exploration of dysfunction and trauma. Through their actions and presence, they help build the psychological horror elements that make the game's story so compelling and disturbing.
